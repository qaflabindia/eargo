# Underwriting Manual

The consumer-lending underwriting manual: the reference material behind
grading and decisions. The runtime cites the section it relied on.

## Section 2.1 -- Risk grades

Risk grades run from A (strongest) to E (weakest). Grade A pairs an
excellent score band with a low debt-to-income band; grade B allows one of
the two to be merely good or moderate; grade C is any profile at the
margin of both; grades D and E carry a poor score band, a high
debt-to-income band, or an existing default.

## Section 4.2 -- Marginal applicants

A grade C applicant whose debt-to-income band is not low must be declined,
whatever the stated purpose of the loan. A grade C applicant with a low
debt-to-income band and no existing defaults may be approved at the
underwriter's judgment, with the decisive factor named.

## Section 5.3 -- Customer communication

Decision notes must state the outcome in the first sentence, name the main
reason in plain terms an applicant can act on, and never disclose internal
scores, bands or grades.
